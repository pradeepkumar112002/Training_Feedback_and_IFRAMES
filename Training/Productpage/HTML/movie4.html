Wakanda Forever is a 2021 superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character Black Panther. It is the sequel to the 2018 film "Black Panther" and is set in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). The film is directed by Ryan Coogler and stars Letitia Wright, Lupita Nyong'o, Danai Gurira, Winston Duke, and others reprising their roles from the first film. In Wakanda Forever, the story focuses on the Wakandan kingdom after the death of King T'Challa (played by Chadwick Boseman), the titular Black Panther, as the surviving members of the Wakandan royal family and the Dora Milaje, Wakanda's all-female special forces, work to protect their land and people from new threats. The film explores themes of tradition, leadership, and legacy, and features an ensemble cast of characters from across the MCU. Wakanda Forever received positive reviews for its handling of the legacy of the first film, its world-building, and its cast of strong, complex characters. The film was a box office success and was praised for its representation of the African diaspora and its celebration of African culture and heritage.
